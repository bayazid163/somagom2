Heritage Preservation Network

Authenticity is our Ancestry.

Somagom isn't just a marketplace; it is a movement. We exist to protect the Geographical Indication (GI) status of Bangladesh's most iconic treasures—from the mist-soaked looms of Sonargaon to the clay-rich kitchens of Bogura.

Beyond the Label

Every product on this platform carries a story that spans centuries. When you hold a **Dhakai Jamdani**, you aren't just holding fabric; you are holding a weaving tradition that survived the colonial era and the test of time.

We realized that in the modern market, "Handmade" had become a marketing buzzword. True artisans were being overshadowed by mass-produced copies that lacked the soul and the specific regional quality of the original. **Somagom was founded in 2024** to solve this—to create a digital sanctuary where the provenance of every item is verified and the lineage of every weaver is respected.

Our team travels to the remote corners of Bangladesh—the Hilsa hubs of Chandpur, the silk villages of Rajshahi, and the pottery wheels of Dhamrai—to ensure that what reaches you is 100% authentic and ethically sourced.

"We weave not just with thread, but with the memories of our fathers."

— Master Weaver, Tangail

The GI Philosophy

Why local origins define global quality

01

The Soil

A Geographical Indication is tied to the earth. The mineral-rich silt of the Padma gives the Ilish its fat; the humid air of Narayanganj allows the Jamdani thread to stay supple. Without the soil, there is no soul.

02

The Skill

Inherited mastery cannot be replicated by machines. Our artisans possess "muscle memory" passed down through seven generations, ensuring motifs and flavors that automated factories can never mimic.

03

The Shield

Your purchase acts as a protective shield. By choosing certified GI products, you prevent legal copyright theft of our national culture and ensure that artisan communities thrive economically for years to come.

How We Verify

01

On-Ground Inspection

We personally visit the production units to ensure they are within the GI-defined geographical boundary.

02

Purity Certification

Every batch of food is tested for harmful additives, and textiles are checked for authentic yarn counts.

03

Traceable Supply Chain

We use direct logistics to remove middlemen, ensuring the artisan receives the maximum profit share.

04

UNESCO Alignment

We prioritize items recognized as Intangible Cultural Heritage, preserving them for future generations.

A Note from the Collective

"We didn't just want to build an e-commerce store. We wanted to build a digital museum where you could actually own the exhibits. Whether it's the silver shine of a Padma Ilish or the intricate thread-work of a Nakshi Kantha, these items are the fingerprints of our national identity."

— The Somagom Team
